About Loes voor Dieren

The Loes voor Dieren is a non-profit foundation that aims to promote animal welfare. This is being achieved by providing animals in need with a foster home, where they are cared until they find a permanent home. The foundation also aims to educate animal owners about proper animal care.
Only animals that are healthy are placed into new homes.
All cats shown on this web page are the property of Loes voor Dieren foundation.
We are just one of a few foster families for cats! We are deeply in love with our cats and would like to share their stories here with you.
If you would like to know more about the foundation or adopt an animal, visit an oficial Loes voor Dieren website.

Why to adopt?

Why should you adopt a cat from the LOES voor Dieren foundation?

  • All cats are fully vaccinated, dewormed and flea treated.
  • All male cats are neutered.
  • All female cats are sterilized.
  • All cats have undergone a health check before placing.
  • We will determine which cat best suits you and your situation.
  • If necessary you will be guided through the care of your new cat.
  • All cats have been chipped. Should your cat run away and be found, it can be quickly returned to you by chip registration.
  • If the cat does not get used to you, the animal can always return to the foundation.

If you adopt a cat from Loes voor Dieren, we ask for a contribution towards the costs that we have incurred. The coordinators can tell you more about the amount.

How to care for your cat

The first day in a new house is strange. The animal may have no appetite or a little loose stool due to nervousness. If this happens, try to talk softly to your cat to calm it down. If the animal is clearly in distress, do not hesitate and go to the vet in consultation with the LOES Foundation.

When you come home with your new family member, make sure the litter box is ready with a valerian drop in it. Allow the cat to explore the new environment. Some cats find a covered litter box scary. In this case, leave the cover of the box for a while until the animal gets used to it. On the first day place food near the litter box, so it can be easily found by the cat.

Make sure you and the cat have time to get used to each other. Always have a scratching post and/or scratching board available so that your furniture is not destroyed. If necessary, you can use a few drops of valerian to encourage the cat to use the scratching post.

If you have other pets, make sure they ate before introducing the new housemate. Provide their own food bowls and if possible a separate litter box during the first period. If you give all animals a valerian drop between the shoulder blades, each animal smells the same and they can handle the situation better. If you have a dog, walk the dog while new cat is exploring the house. When a dog comes home from his walk, the newcomer is already there and has found a safe place.

Do not let the animal out until it is used to you and the new environment, this can take up to 4-6 weeks. Go out with the animal the first few times and use a cat leash if necessary.

A cat also needs exercise, especially indoor cats tend to become overweight. Provide your cats with various toys, don't forget the most beloved toy – laser pointer! Just be careful and do not shine it into your cat eyes.

Snoopy was present to Ellen (foster mother for Loes voor Dieren) for her birthday. At that time Snoopy was only 10 weeks old and she quickly became good friends with the other two house cats Baloe and Caddy. 5.5 years ago Ellen has decided to quit smoking. And that was not an easy task, so she needed a distraction. One day she saw extremely cute pictures of baby Banjer and thought that if she buys him, she is going to be too busy taking care of him to even think about smoking. Did this tactic work? Yes! Piccolo

Name Gender Color Age
Piccolo Female Grey with dark stripes 5

In 2015 the family officially started working with Loes voor Dieren and fostering cats. Piccolo was from the first nest of cats. Her story starts really sad, as a baby she was found in the street being comatose. The chances of her surviving were slim. It took long time for her to recover, of course she did survive, but her growth was affected by it. She was named Picollo due to her being extremely small. Even now, being 5 years old, she still looks like a little baby. Picollo needs a lot of care and attention, as she have also suffered brain damage. But that does not make her any worse pet to have! Once she gets used to human, she becomes really cuddly. When she is laying in her spot and sees you coming, she instantly jumps from happiness and waits for her deserved pets.

Sisco

Name Gender Color Age
Sisco Male Black stripes with gray-brown 4

Sisco was found on the street, together with his mother and two other siblings. His brother died shortly after, but sister Lola has survived. He was very, very sick and had an eye infection. For a very long it was unknown if Sisco was even able to see anything. During one of the check ups at the vet it was decided that it is best to remove his eyes, as there were inflammation that could have not been treated. After the surgery, he quickly got used to the surroundings. Taking care of blind cat took a lot of time, as he had to be trained to follow various instructions. Sisco`s story may sound sad, but now he is a very happy and very active cat. He knows everything in the house, he can still bravely climbs on tall scratching posts, takes care of little kittens and plays with his sister Lola.

Lola

Name Gender Color Age
Lola Female Turtle, random patches of black,brown 4

Lola is the sister of Sisco. Luckily for her, she was not as sick and the eye infection affected only one eye. You might have read a lot that animals don`t have a sense of family, but the case of these two sibling cats could prove it wrong. If Sisco is in distress, Lola is the first one to run to him, she always takes care of him and if needed does everything to calm him down. Every evening they play together. They share a very deep bond and they could never be separated. Lola, being typical female, is very jealous! Even towards the little kittens. Over time her jealousy does calm down and she starts developing motherly feelings towards little kittens. Lola is also very close with her boyfriend Banjer. They are the cutest couple in the house, every time they sleep together, you see the little paws of Banjer hugging Lola.

Donut

Donut Male White with brown spots 3

Donut comes from a family of five kittens. Three of five kittens, including Donut were really sick. They used to get special drops into their eyes a few times a day. This made Donut dislike being petted on his head. He can be a very sweet cat, but not many people want a cat that does not want to be petted. Of course over time (very long time) he forms a trust with humans and does not mind being petted but he still prefers to be by himself. Now Donut looks like a traditional Dutch donut, better known as oliebollen. As we have so many cats in the house, placing him on needed diet is not really possible. We are hoping that one day he will find a loving family, who can give him all the love he truly deserves.

Butler was found when he was only one week old and all alone. At his back paw he is missing a toe. This might suggest that he was taken away from a cat nest by another animal. He was bottle fed. Abbey

Name Gender Color Age
Abbey Female Black with some white spots 2

Abbey was found in a big family of cats with no people around. She was very malnourished, scared of people. Even though Abbey now for 2 years lives in our house, she is still afraid of people. Some cats if found too late already are feral and cannot form a bond with humans. Sometimes you can pet her and she does enjoy it! But being too afraid quickly runs away. We would love to find her a loving new home, but she is still not ready to be placed. Every time we get a new litter of small kittens, she by observing them is learning more and more about people. That gives us hope that one day she could learn how to trust humans.

Freekje

Freekje Male Black with white spots 8 months

Freekje is named in honor of Ellen's life partner Freek. Freekje was found on Frederik van Eeden street just three days before Freek's death. He was found with two sisters, soaking wet in the rain. At that time they were only one day old! On their bellies the umbilical cord was still attached, letting us know that someone threw them away right after birth. All 3 kittens were bottle fed and were growing up really quickly! The two sisters after sterilisation were adopted and went to live in their new homes. And Freekje, due to sentimental values is staying with us. He is a very playful and happy kitten. Currently, he is taking very good care of our five little kittens! He is teaching them how to play, he is washing them (as they are still too little to do it by themselves) and protecting them from other cats.
